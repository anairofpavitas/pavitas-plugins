# Clue patterns: venue-game palette

This is a starting palette of mechanics that pick the next venue in a two-stage clue. Riff on it. Invent new ones. Don't reuse a mechanic within a single hunt.

The list is ordered by default preference. Reach for player-internal first. Mix in place-driven. Use stranger-driven sparingly.

## Player-internal (default — most clues should come from here)

- **Coin flip.** Heads one direction, tails the other. Walk until the first qualifying shop.
- **Rock-paper-scissors.** Loser picks from three options you name.
- **First-letter race.** Use a player's initial; first qualifying shop wins.
- **Initial alphabet walk.** Walk until the first shop whose name starts with the player's initial.
- **Blind map-pin.** Drop a pin on the map with eyes closed within a set radius.
- **Camera-roll vibe match.** Open the most recent photo. Match its dominant color or vibe to a shop window within sight.
- **Direction nobody came from.** Walk that way until you hit a business.
- **Secret writes.** Each player secretly writes a shop. Both visits happen.
- **Number-of-the-block.** Roll a die or pick a random number 1–10. Walk that many storefronts past the current one.
- **Dealer's choice.** One player picks for the group with no input — different player each round.

## Player-to-place (good middle ground)

- **Window two-fer.** Find a window that contains two of the day's creative seeds at once. Go in.
- **Sign-driven.** Walk until you find a hand-lettered sign, chalkboard, or sandwich board. Go in.
- **Architecture pick.** Pick the next building with a feature matching a seed (a color, a texture, a material). Go in or to the closest shop adjacent.
- **Smell-driven.** First shop where you can smell something from the sidewalk.
- **Sound-driven.** Walk toward music or noise until you find its source. Go in if possible, adjacent shop if not.
- **Animal magnet.** Walk to the next dog or cat you see. Whichever shop is closest, that's the venue.

## Player-to-stranger (use sparingly — one or two per hunt, max)

Only deploy when the group's energy is up for it, the neighborhood has foot traffic, and it's not late evening. Skip entirely if anyone in the group reads as uncomfortable approaching strangers, or if the area is sparse.

- **Color-tagged stranger.** Ask the next stranger wearing a specific color where they last bought something specific.
- **Shopkeeper recommendation.** Ask the shopkeeper at the current venue for a recommendation. Go there next.
- **Hand-pick.** Ask one shopkeeper to hand-pick something for the project. Their call wins.
- **Local secret.** Ask one stranger for their favorite shop in the neighborhood. Go.

## Adaptation by mode

- **Competitive.** Race the mechanic. First to the venue picks first, or score goes to whoever buys faster / better.
- **Cooperative.** Consult the mechanic together. Group decides.
- **Teams.** Send a runner per team. Each team's mechanic is independent.
- **Parallel-solo.** Each player rolls their own mechanic and goes their own direction. Regroup at checkpoints.

## Fallbacks

Every mechanic needs a fallback in the clue text — what to do if the first lead busts. The fallback should still feel hunt-flavored, not generic ("the next shop in the same direction" works most places; "skip to the optional sidequest in your pocket" works when the group has one banked).
