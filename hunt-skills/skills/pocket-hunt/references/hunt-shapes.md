# Hunt shapes

Three shapes define the overall structure of a hunt. The shape is asked (or inferred) at intake.

---

## Open exploration

**No fixed endpoint. The hunt unfolds where the user is.**

Default shape. The user is in or near a place and wants to engage with it. Clue mechanics generate venues and paths within the stated range.

- **Range:** block, neighborhood, or wider area as user specifies
- **Closure:** named end state (rung 4 with a project) or time-up (play-by-ear)
- **Path-aware clues:** used when there's distance to cover

**When to use:**
- The user has time and no specific destination
- They want the hunt to determine where they end up
- Most rung 1 and rung 2 hunts
- Most "let's see what we find" energy

**Generation tip:** anchor the first clue near where the user is starting. Don't send them to the far edge of the range for the first beat — that's a slog before the hunt feels like a hunt.

---

## Travel hunt

**The user has a destination they're heading to, with time to explore the route. The hunt happens en route, with final quests at the destination.**

This is the "I'm going to Andersonville eventually, but I have a couple hours and want to make the journey count" shape.

- **Range:** the route from current location to destination, plus reasonable detours along it
- **Closure:** arrival at the destination, with final clue(s) completed there
- **Path-aware clues:** essential. Most clues will have path beats — what to notice or do between current point and next venue.

**Structure:**
1. **First clue stretch:** anchor at current location, set the seeds with prompts grounded in here
2. **Middle clue stretches:** progress toward destination, with detour latitude. Side quests common.
3. **Approach clue:** transition into destination — *"You're close to Andersonville now. The next clue happens within Andersonville proper."*
4. **Destination clues (1–3):** final acquisitions, the project completion, the big find. These are the hunt's main beats and the destination earns them.
5. **Closing:** at or near destination, with optional sit-down at a cafe/bar/park to wrap

**Travel time matters:** ask roughly how long the user wants to be in transit vs. how long at destination. A 60-min walk to Andersonville is different from a 20-min drive. Adjust clue count and pace accordingly.

**When to use:**
- Named destination known in advance
- User wants the journey to be the practice, not just the means
- Often combinatorial (rung 4) — gathering on the way, completing at destination

---

## Destination-with-delivery

**The hunt ends at a specific person's location. The gift/finds get delivered to them.**

The most socially-charged shape. The hunt is structured around a person and ends with arriving at their door (or making contact). The combinatorial gift bag is the most-developed example, but variations exist:

- **Gift bag for [name]** — 5–7 small finds combined into a presentation, delivered to their home or saved for next meeting.
- **Meal-share** — gathering food items en route to a meal at someone's place.
- **Workshop arrival** — gathering inputs for a thing you're going to make with someone when you get there.
- **Surprise visit with a hand-crafted token** — one carefully chosen object, arriving unannounced.

- **Range:** route to the destination person's location, plus detours
- **Closure:** the delivery itself
- **Path-aware clues:** essential, with social-context overlays

**Generation tips:**

- **Anchor in the recipient.** First clue stretch establishes who they are — what they like, what season of life they're in, what would land. The seeds can be the recipient: *"think about [recipient]. What's a color they wear, a texture they're drawn to, a thing they've mentioned wanting? Those are your seeds."*
- **Path adapts to recipient.** *"On the way to [recipient]'s place, find: [list of roles for the gift bag]."*
- **Closure is the delivery.** The skill doesn't write the delivery beat for the user — it just frames the moment. *"You're a block away. The gift is assembled. Knock, or text first. Either's fine."*
- **Aftermath check.** *"How'd it go?"* — light, curious, no scoring.

**When to use:**
- The user has a person in mind nearby
- They want social stakes in the outing
- They have time for a multi-find hunt (typically 90+ min)
- Rung 4 (combinatorial) almost always

**Show up unannounced vs. text ahead:** don't ask. Let the user decide in the moment. The skill just prompts: *"Either knock unannounced, or text first to see if they're around. Your call."*

**If recipient isn't home:** the gift survives for next meeting. Note it in the closing.

## Shape transitions mid-hunt

The user can switch shape mid-hunt:

- **Open → Travel:** *"Actually I want to end up in Andersonville."* Skill pivots: remaining clues become path-aware toward Andersonville.
- **Open → Destination-with-delivery:** *"I just realized [friend] lives 3 blocks from here — want to drop something off."* Skill pivots: next clue stretch becomes a small gift-bag mini-hunt with their place as endpoint.
- **Travel → Open:** *"I'm not going to Andersonville anymore — let's just stay around here."* Skill drops the destination, keeps the seeds and finds, continues open.
- **Travel → Destination-with-delivery:** *"Andersonville is where [friend] is — let me bring them something."* Skill adds the social dimension to the final clue stretch.

Accept all of these without resistance. Pivots are features, not failures.

---

## Side quests within shapes

Side quests (see `side-quests.md`) work in any shape but trigger differently:

- **Open exploration:** side quests can happen anywhere, occasional rotation
- **Travel hunt:** side quests preferred on route, less in destination zone (don't dilute the destination beats)
- **Destination-with-delivery:** side quests rare — the recipient is the main social beat already, don't crowd it

If a side quest naturally aligns with the current shape (e.g., "person you know in the area" while open-exploring near someone's neighborhood), surface it as an offer. The user can accept and pivot the shape entirely, or accept it as a side quest within the current shape.
