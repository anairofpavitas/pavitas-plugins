# Parallel activity

A parallel activity is something the user is carrying with them and might do during the hunt — independent of the hunt mechanics but capable of being woven in. Common examples:

- Crochet, knitting, or other portable handcraft
- Sketchbook or watercolor kit
- Journal or notebook
- Film camera (with the slower, more deliberate pace it implies)
- Audio recorder (capturing sound seeds, field recordings)
- A book they're reading

Ask at intake: *"Are you bringing a project or activity with you — crochet, sketchbook, journal, anything else?"* Default is none. If yes, the skill adapts.

---

## How parallel activity changes the hunt

Three integration points:

### 1. Pit stops become activity sessions

Standard pit stop: rest, hydrate, snack. Parallel-activity pit stop: rest + 15–30 min with the activity. The clue text shifts:

> *"Pit stop. Find a bench, cafe patio, or quiet spot. Sit for 20 minutes with your crochet. Work whatever's on your hook. Don't push for progress — just stitch."*

These work especially well in rung 1 and rung 2 (noticing/capture). The activity becomes the deepening of presence rather than a parallel task.

### 2. Seeds feed the activity

Photos and noticings can become inspiration for the parallel activity. For crochet specifically:

- **Color seed → colorway.** "The rust-orange you photographed becomes a candidate for your next yarn."
- **Texture seed → stitch.** "The brick texture you noticed suggests a textured stitch — popcorns, bobbles, or a slip-stitch pattern."
- **Shape seed → motif.** "The leaf silhouettes you spotted are motif candidates."
- **Mood seed → drape.** "The mood you named suggests a fabric weight — heavy and grounded, or open and airy?"

The skill can fold seed-translation directly into clue text:

> *"On your next pit stop, look at the photos from seeds 1 and 2. What yarn weights and stitch textures do they suggest for the [current crochet project]? Make a small note in your project journal, or just stitch from that intuition for the next 20 minutes."*

For sketchbook/painting: same logic. Color and texture seeds become palette and gesture inputs. For journal: seeds become writing prompts (handled by the user in their own journal, not in the chat).

### 3. End-of-hunt: the activity is part of the keepsake

For rung 2+, the parallel activity is part of the closing. If the user did 30 minutes of crochet during the hunt, that's *part of what they came home with*. Acknowledge it in the closing:

> *"You did 25 minutes of crochet on the bench in Portage Park. You photographed 3 seeds and gathered 4 found objects from the path. The hunt landed somewhere different than you started — you're coming home with the start of a new colorway and a small botanical."*

## Activity-specific adaptations

### Crochet (Pavi's default)

- Pit stops are 15–30 min, ideally on a bench or cafe patio with steady light
- The walking sections are stitch-rest — hands are free, eyes are on the world
- Seeds bias toward colors, textures, drape inspirations
- Hook size and yarn weight implications are valid closing outputs (the skill can suggest "based on what you saw, your next project leans bulky weight in cool tones")
- For Pavi specifically: project context can come from his current WIP (Sniegs Snow Sweater in Aubergine Lamb's Pride Bulky as of last memory)

### Sketchbook / watercolor

- Pit stops are 10–25 min, ideally with a view to sketch from
- Seeds bias toward composition, line, light
- Photos can become reference for future studio work
- Field-painting requires more setup time; clue mechanics should account for it

### Journal

- Pit stops are 10–15 min, can happen anywhere with seating
- Seeds bias toward mood, memory, association
- The user writes off-app — the skill never asks to see what they wrote
- Particularly compatible with rung 1 (notice) and conversation seeds

### Film camera

- Slower walking pace, more deliberate framing
- Photos can't be shared into chat until developed — so seed photos are described in text instead
- The hunt becomes a roll-of-film hunt — 24 or 36 frames, each one earned

### Audio recorder

- Sound seeds become primary
- Pit stops are field-recording sessions
- The keepsake is the audio compilation, not photos

### Book

- Pit stops are reading breaks (10–20 min)
- The book itself can seed: a phrase, an image, a character mood becomes a hunt anchor
- Particularly compatible with rung 1 noticing and solo hunts

## When parallel activity overshadows the hunt

If the user signals that the activity is the main event and the hunt is the wrapper (*"I really just want to walk and crochet — give me a light hunt"*), shift mode: bias toward rung 1 or 2, more frequent pit stops, fewer clues. The hunt becomes a structure for the activity rather than the other way around. Honor that.

Conversely, if the activity feels like it's getting in the way (the user starts skipping pit stops, won't sit), suggest dropping it: *"You've been on the move — want to skip the next pit stop and keep hunting?"* Don't enforce activity if it's not serving.

---

## Multiple parallel activities

The user might carry more than one (crochet + journal, or sketchbook + camera). At intake, just note all of them. Pit stops can rotate or stack — *"Sit for 20 minutes. Stitch for the first 10, sketch or journal for the second 10."* User picks the split if asked.
