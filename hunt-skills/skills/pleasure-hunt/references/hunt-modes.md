# Hunt modes

Four modes for who's involved. Set at intake. Each shapes the clues, the escalation, the equipment, and the risks.

---

## Solo

The user alone. The simplest mode and a strong default.

- **Clues.** Self-pleasure, equipment, edging, gooning, goal practice. Pacing and denial play well — a solo hunt can be a slow build across an afternoon.
- **Equipment.** The richest mode for toys. The kit is the partner.
- **Escalation.** Bounded only by the user's own comfort, which can move freely. No consent gate but their own.
- **Apps.** Dating apps can add a remote spark without leaving the hunt solo — see "Dating apps" below.
- **Goals.** The home of the *alone* goal variants — multiple ejaculations, anal orgasms, gooning stretches, chastity wear.

## Partner

With Colin (the default partner — adjust if the user names someone else).

- **Clues.** Mutual, escalating. Signal mechanics matter: a way to start a clue without breaking the moment to talk — a touch, a code word, a sent emoji.
- **The foreplay build.** Partner mode's signature flavor — an all-day string of clues that keep making low-key sexual contact and escalate toward sex. See `clue-patterns.md`.
- **Consent.** Colin's enthusiasm is part of the engine. Every partner clue carries a softer fallback so it survives a no.
- **Goals.** The *together* variants, and the open-conversations-with-Colin goal.

## Group

Three or more — a threesome, a play group, a bathhouse trip with company.

- **Clues.** Simpler and looser than partner clues. A group's dynamics can't be scripted — give the user objectives, not choreography.
- **Consent.** The highest-stakes mode for it. Everyone involved is a yes that can change. Clues are invitations to the user, never instructions for the group.
- **The elegant exit.** When the group setting isn't itself the sex — a party, a dinner, a trip with friends — the hunt's last beat is getting out cleanly: a reason to leave, a place to go. *"Find a place to have sex within 10 minutes. Not near home? Get creative."* Generate the exit as a real clue, with its own clock and an escape hatch.
- **Goals.** Threesomes, bathhouse, being a cum dump — the *together* or group variants.

## Cruising

Out looking for new contact, in person or through apps. The hunt is the *search* as much as the sex.

- **Clues.** Two strands — *finding* (scanning a space, working an app, catching an eye) and, if it lands, *doing*. Finding clues fire regardless; doing clues carry escape hatches.
- **Where.** Bars, bathhouses, sex clubs, cruising spots, the woods, the gym — wherever the user is. Apps work anywhere.
- **Consent and safety.** Strangers. A clue can lightly note telling someone where you are, or meeting in a semi-public spot first. One phrase, not a lecture.
- **Discretion.** The user is discreet — clues never push exposure.
- **Goals.** Public sex, bathhouse, woods sex, being a cum dump, exhibitionism — the *alone* variants often live here.

## Dating apps

For solo and cruising hunts, dating and messaging apps are fair game. The user is **discreet** in how they use them — light suggestions only, never a directive to expose themselves or be reckless.

Apps that count: Grindr, Sniffies, and sexy contacts on Snapchat, Instagram, or iMessage.

App clues come in two shapes:

**Spark clues** — a remote charge without necessarily meeting:

> *Message three people and ask to see their cock. Send something of yours first if you want to.*

> *Open Grindr. Find someone you'd actually want to do the next clue with, and send it to them.*

**Find clues** — looking for a real-world meet:

> *Take five minutes to find someone you like on an app. Can you get them to want in? If one says yes, the hunt is on — you have to follow through.*

> *On Sniffies, find someone within a 10-minute walk. If they're up for it, go.*

App clues always have an out: if no one bites in the time given, the clue passes and the hunt continues. The search is the clue; a yes is a bonus, not a requirement.

## Mixed and shifting modes

A hunt can start one mode and become another — a solo cruising hunt that turns into a partner hunt when someone says yes, a partner hunt that opens into a group hunt with a third. Generate for the mode the user names, but a cruising or app clue landing *is* a mode shift. Write the later clues loose enough to survive it, or name the pivot in the clue: *"If this turns into more than a quick thing, the rest of the hunt is yours to run."*
