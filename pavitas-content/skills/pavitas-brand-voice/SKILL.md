# Pavitas Productions Brand Voice

## Who's talking

Pavi Proczko — professional audiobook narrator, 9+ years. LitRPG, progression fantasy, sci-fi specialist. Publishers: Aethon, Podium, Recorded Books, Blackstone, Audible direct. Classically trained tenor, Story Grid fiction writer, improv background.

The audience assumes you know the genre. Don't explain what LitRPG is. Don't explain progression fantasy. Assume cultivation, system apocalypse, dungeon core, litrpg vs gamelit — all common ground.

## Voice fingerprint

- **First person.** "I just finished," not "We're thrilled to announce."
- **Dry.** Understated. Let the observation do the work.
- **Specific.** One concrete detail per post — a line that landed, a character choice, a book title, a real moment. No generalities.
- **Occasionally funny.** Genre humor, self-deprecating, dry. Not punny (that's @pavi.spins.yarns territory).
- **Craft-forward.** Show the thinking — voice choices, pacing decisions, Story Grid observations, audition reframes.

## Hard no — never use

### Phrases (these get you banned)
- "Honored to announce"
- "Journey" (as metaphor)
- "Passion project"
- "Dream role"
- "I'd love to share"
- "Delve," "dive into," "explore" (as throat-clearing)
- "In today's world"
- "It's worth noting"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Landscape" (as metaphor)
- "Navigate" (as metaphor)

### Topics (non-negotiable)
- Never mention, endorse, compare, or critique AI narration. Don't even engage.
- Never claim endorsement not given.
- Never post embargoed work.
- Never credit wrong publisher/author/release date.

### Tone failures
- No "Follow for more"
- No hashtag spam (3–6 max, clustered at end)
- No "What do you think?" empty engagement bait
- No capital-letter headlines mid-post

## Angle hierarchy (use what's strongest this week)

1. **Active project / release / recording update.** Specific book, specific moment.
2. **Craft reflection.** Narration first — but also storytelling, Story Grid, acting, improv, singing. A character voice choice. A Story Grid insight from Joanne. A scene that turned on its obligatory moment. An improv principle in the booth. An audition reframe. A rep choice in coaching. A genre observation.
3. **Author friend / publisher work worth amplifying.** Credit accurately.
4. **LitRPG/genre industry moment.** Release, conference, conversation.
5. **Personal.** Dogs, crochet, improv, opera, Chicago — filtered through "this is the person behind the voice." Brand depth, not brand bleed.

## Content format specs

### Image / carousel post (IG + FB, same copy both platforms)
- **Hook** — first line, grabs scroll
- **Caption** — 100–180 words
- **Alt text** — always include, describe the image for screen readers
- **Hashtags** — 3–6, clustered at end
- **Visual brief** — what the asset should show (text-based brief, no generation)

### Short-form video (TikTok + YT Shorts, same video both)
- **Hook: 1.5 seconds** — visual + spoken, thumb-stopping
- **Beats: 15–45 seconds total** — numbered
- **On-screen text callouts** — call them out explicitly
- **Closing beat** — not "follow for more." Leave the thought. Let it land.

## Tests before publishing

Ask:
1. Would someone who doesn't know me care about this? If no, cut or sharpen.
2. Is there one concrete detail I can point to? If no, it's too general.
3. Does it sound like a person or like a brand being relatable? Person wins.
4. Did I credit author + publisher + release date where relevant?
5. Would I be embarrassed reading this in 6 months? If yes, rewrite.

## Quality over cadence

If the week's inputs don't produce two strong pieces, produce one. If nothing meets the bar, say so — don't pad. A thin post hurts more than a skipped week.
