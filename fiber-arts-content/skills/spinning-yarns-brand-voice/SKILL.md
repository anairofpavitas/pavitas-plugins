# Spinning Yarns Brand Voice

@pavi.spins.yarns is a fiber-arts-first account that happens to belong to an audiobook narrator. The narration identity is a thread, not the frame.

## Who the account is

- **Brand name:** Spinning Yarns with Pavi
- **Handle:** @pavi.spins.yarns (IG + FB)
- **Name:** Pavi Proczko
- **Lane:** editorial garment crochet, Tunisian, interlocking/reversible stitches, indie dyer yarn, Chicago LYS community

Pavi is an advanced crocheter. He's written patterns, he teaches, he knows the technique landscape. Voice reflects that — not a beginner, not a guru, a practitioner with opinions.

## Voice principles

**Warm, witty, genuine.** Write like a friend showing you what's on the hook, not a brand announcing content.

**Process-forward.** Show the making, not just the finished object. Row counts, stitch choices, what's working, what isn't.

**Specific over general.** Always one concrete detail: yarn brand + weight + colorway, hook size, designer name, pattern title, stitch count, where it's challenging.

**Pun-friendly.** It's a crochet account called Spinning Yarns. Puns are permitted, occasionally required.

**Pro-crochet on its own terms.** Crochet is beautiful as crochet. Never frame it as "passing for knitting." Never pay that compliment, never accept it uncritically.

**Narrator thread, not frame.** The overlap with audiobook work shows up when it's real — meditative repetition, voice/rhythm parallels, recording-to-making transitions. Don't force it.

## Never use

- **AI narration mentions** — positive, negative, neutral. None.
- **AI-generated pattern promotion** — never boost, recommend, or endorse.
- **"Journey"** as a metaphor for a project or craft arc.
- **"Delve," "landscape," "navigate"** — AI tells.
- **"In today's world," "it's worth noting," "let's dive in"** — copy padding.
- **"You can't even tell it's not knitted"** — or any flattened version of that compliment. If someone else says it in comments, the reply is gracious but corrective.
- **Corporate jargon** — "synergy," "leverage," "innovative," "passion project."
- **Empty hype** — "absolutely stunning," "obsessed," "literally dying."

## Structural style

- First person, active voice, contractions fine.
- Short sentences. Active voice.
- One concrete detail per post, minimum.
- Hook line first, then 2–4 sentences of substance, then one call-to-engagement (a question, a "tell me about your current WIP").
- Match length to platform norms — IG captions 80–160 words.
- Hashtags clustered at end: broad (2–3) + technique (2–3) + project-specific (2–3).

## Content type patterns

### Work In Progress (WIP)
- Photo/video of current project on the hook
- Caption about the process — what stitch, what's working, what's challenging
- Yarn + hook + designer credit
- Connect to daily life when natural ("recording all morning, hook all evening")

### Finished Object (FO)
- Glamour shots of completed piece
- Pattern credit + yarn details + hook + finished dimensions if relevant
- Brief story — what drew him to the pattern, what the making taught him

### Technique Spotlight
- Close-up of a specific stitch or technique (Tunisian sim st, interlocking, mosaic)
- Educational but conversational
- Good for short-form video — less common techniques draw engagement

### Designer Amplification
- When a designer in his lane drops something worth knowing about
- Credit them clearly, tag their handle, link their pattern
- Add his own take — why it's interesting, where it sits in the technique landscape
- Never make it about him when it's about them

### LYS / Community
- Chicago shops he actually uses (Nina Chicago, Sister-Arts Studio, Third Coast Stitches, Knit 1 Chicago, Firefly Fiber Arts)
- LYS Day, events, classes, fiber gatherings

### Narrator Crossover
- The overlap, not the advertisement
- Good: "There's a rhythm to stitching that mirrors the rhythm of narration — breath, pause, repeat."
- Bad: "When I'm not in the booth, I'm on the hook!" (flat, markety)

## Quality checks before posting

1. Is there one concrete detail? (yarn/hook/designer/row/stitch)
2. Does the hook line stop the scroll in under 10 words?
3. Is the call-to-engagement specific or generic?
4. Are all credits in (designer, yarn, retailer if relevant)?
5. Does it sound like a human wrote it?
6. Any AI-tell words snuck in? (delve, landscape, journey)
7. Is the crochet framed on its own terms?
