## 1. Year-to-Live Clarifier

**When to use**: Major life decisions, priority-setting, when feeling lost or overwhelmed

**Template**:

```markdown
# Year-to-Live Decision: [Decision Name]

## The Setup
Today's date: [DATE]
My last day: [DATE + 1 YEAR]
Time remaining: [DAYS/WEEKS/MONTHS]

## The Brutal Question
If I only had [TIME REMAINING] left to live, would I spend ANY time on this?

**Honest answer**: [YES/NO/MAYBE]

## The Priority Stack
Knowing my time is limited, here's what ACTUALLY matters:

1. **Non-negotiables** (Things I'd do even with 1 week left)
   - [Item with specific time allocation]
   - [Item with specific time allocation]

2. **Important but flexible** (Things I'd do with 6+ months)
   - [Item with specific time allocation]
   - [Item with specific time allocation]

3. **Nice to have** (Things I'd skip entirely)
   - [Item to eliminate or delegate]
   - [Item to eliminate or delegate]

## This Decision Specifically
**Does this decision serve my non-negotiables?**: [YES/NO]
**If yes, how?**: [Specific connection]
**If no, why am I considering it?**: [Honest answer]

## The Counterfactual
On my last day, will I regret:
- **Saying YES to this**: [What I'd regret]
- **Saying NO to this**: [What I'd regret]

## Decision
**I will**: [CLEAR YES/NO/DEFER]
**Because**: [One-sentence reason rooted in non-negotiables]

## 6-Month Check-in Trigger
[Set reminder for DATE] - Review: Did I honor this decision? What did I learn?
```

## 2. Narrative Reality Check

**When to use**: When you have a story about yourself that might not match reality (productivity patterns, behavior habits, capabilities)

**Template**:

```markdown
# Narrative Reality Check: [Decision Name]

## My Story
**What I tell myself**: [Write your narrative in full detail]

Example: "I'm most productive at 5am. If I just wake up early, I'll get so much more done. I've always been a morning person."

## The Evidence Audit
Now let's check what ACTUALLY happens:

**Calendar review**: [Pull last 30 days of calendar data]
- When do I actually schedule focused work?
- When do I actually complete tasks?
- What patterns emerge?

**Email/communication patterns**: [Check timestamps]
- When am I most responsive?
- When do I send the most thoughtful emails?
- What does this reveal?

**Past behavior**: [Specific examples]
- Last time I tried this, what happened?
- How long did it last?
- What broke the pattern?

## The Gap Analysis
**Story**: [Summary of narrative]
**Reality**: [Summary of evidence]
**Gap**: [Honest assessment of the difference]

## The Adjusted Narrative
Based on evidence, here's what's ACTUALLY true:
[Rewrite the narrative based on data]

Example: "I'm most productive between 9am-12pm based on 30 days of data. While I like the IDEA of 5am productivity, I've never sustained it for more than 3 days. My 'morning person' identity is aspirational, not actual."

## Decision Implications
**Original decision based on story**: [What you were going to do]
**Revised decision based on reality**: [What you'll actually do]
**Why this matters**: [How this prevents future pain]

## Reality-Test Commitment
**Next time I tell myself this story, I will**:
- [Specific action to verify with data]
- [Specific metric to check]

## 90-Day Check-in Trigger
[Set reminder for DATE] - Review: Am I still believing the story or living the reality?
```

## 3. 7-Day Cooling-Off Protocol

**When to use**: Impulse purchase decisions, commitments, "limited time offers"

**Template**:

```markdown
# 7-Day Cooling-Off: [Purchase/Decision Name]

**Date started**: [DATE]
**Decision deadline**: [DATE + 7 DAYS]
**Price**: [AMOUNT]
**Discount**: [AMOUNT/PERCENTAGE] (Expires: [DATE])

## The Discount Interrogation

### Would you buy this at full price?
**Answer**: [YES/NO/MAYBE]
**Why this matters**: If no, you don't want the product—you want the feeling of winning.

### How often does this brand run sales?
**Research findings**: [Check past 12 months]
**Pattern identified**: [e.g., "Sale every 6 weeks" or "Always on sale"]
**Implication**: [Is this really urgent?]

### What's the actual $ saved vs opportunity cost?
**Discount amount**: $[X]
**Hours spent researching**: [Y]
**Effective hourly rate of "savings"**: $[X/Y]
**Your actual hourly value**: $[Z]
**Reality check**: [Is this worth your time?]

## The Pattern Analysis
**Similar past purchases**: [List previous impulse buys in this category]

For each:
- Do you still use it?
- Did the "exclusive features" matter?
- What was the trigger? (Discount? Shiny feature? Comparison to others?)

**Pattern identified**: [What do you notice?]

## The Incremental Value Test
**What I already own**: [List current items in this category]
**What they can do**: [Capabilities]
**What this new item adds**: [Specific delta]

**Honest assessment**: 
- Features I'll actually use: [List]
- Features I'll never use: [List]
- Net new capability: [What's truly different?]

**$/Quality-of-Life ratio**: 
- Cost per new capability: $[COST] / [N capabilities] = $[X] per feature
- Expected uses per month: [Realistic estimate]
- Cost per use over 2 years: $[Calculate]

## The Regret Projection

### If I DON'T buy this:
**In 7 days**: [How will I feel?]
**In 6 months**: [How will I feel?]
**In 2 years**: [How will I feel?]

### If I DO buy this:
**In 7 days**: [How will I feel?]
**In 6 months**: [How will I feel?]
**In 2 years**: [How will I feel?]

## Fast-Track Override Option
**ONLY use if genuinely urgent (not just discount-driven)**

If you must decide faster than 7 days:
- [ ] I've answered all questions above honestly
- [ ] I would buy this at full price
- [ ] This provides genuine new capability, not incremental improvement
- [ ] Missing this opportunity creates real cost, not just FOMO
- [ ] I've documented what I'm sacrificing by rushing

**If all boxes checked**, you may decide now. If not, wait the full 7 days.

## Day 7 Decision
**I will**: [BUY/NOT BUY]
**Because**: [One-sentence reason]
**If buying**: Maximum price I'll pay: $[X]

## 30-Day Review Trigger
[Set reminder for DATE] - Review: Do I use this? Did the features matter? What did I learn about my impulse patterns?
```

## 4. Time Allocation Audit

**When to use**: Saying yes to new commitments, quarterly planning, feeling overwhelmed

**Template**:

```markdown
# Time Allocation Audit: [Decision Name]

## The Fantasy Budget
**What I THINK my time looks like**:

| Activity | Hours/Week |
|----------|------------|
| [Activity 1] | [Hours] |
| [Activity 2] | [Hours] |
| Total: | [168 hours] |

## The Reality Audit
**Pull actual data from**:
- Calendar (last 30 days)
- Screen time reports
- Limitless transcripts
- Email timestamps

**What my time ACTUALLY looks like**:

| Activity | Hours/Week | Gap from Fantasy |
|----------|------------|------------------|
| [Activity 1] | [Hours] | +/- [Difference] |
| [Activity 2] | [Hours] | +/- [Difference] |
| Total: | [168 hours] | |

## The Insight Layer
**Biggest surprises**:
1. [What you didn't expect]
2. [What you didn't expect]

**Time sinks** (things taking more time than they're worth):
- [Activity]: [Hours] → [What this costs you]

**Time gems** (things providing outsized value):
- [Activity]: [Hours] → [What this gives you]

## This Decision's True Cost
**What this new commitment requires**: [X hours/week]

**Where will those hours come from?**
| Activity to reduce | Current hours | New hours | What I lose |
|-------------------|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| [Activity] | [X] | [Y] | [Consequence] |

**Be specific**: Not "I'll find time," but "I'll reduce [ACTIVITY] from [X] to [Y] hours"

## The Sustainability Test
**Can I sustain this for 6 months?**
- [ ] I've identified specific hours to trade
- [ ] The trade-off is worth it based on my priorities
- [ ] I've accounted for energy/context-switching costs
- [ ] I have a plan for what I'll stop doing

**If any box is unchecked**: Either revise the plan or decline the commitment.

## Decision
**I will**: [YES/NO/DEFER]
**Time trade**: [What I'll reduce and by how much]
**Review trigger**: [Set 30-day check-in]

## 30-Day Reality Check
[Set reminder for DATE]
- Did I actually make the trade I committed to?
- What was harder than expected?
- What do I need to adjust?
```

## 5. Incremental Value Calculator

**When to use**: Evaluating upgrades, additional features, "better" versions of things you already own

**Template**:

```markdown
# Incremental Value: [Item Name]

## What I Currently Have
**Item**: [Current item]
**Paid**: $[Amount]
**Purchase date**: [Date]
**Current utility** (1-10): [Score]
**Current satisfaction** (1-10): [Score]

## What I'm Considering
**Item**: [New item]
**Price**: $[Amount]
**Key difference**: [Primary selling point]

## Feature Delta Analysis

| Feature | Current Item | New Item | Do I Actually Use This? |
|---------|-------------|----------|------------------------|
| [Feature 1] | [Status] | [Status] | [YES/NO + Evidence] |
| [Feature 2] | [Status] | [Status] | [YES/NO + Evidence] |
| [Feature 3] | [Status] | [Status] | [YES/NO + Evidence] |

## The Brutal Math
**Price of new item**: $[X]
**Resale value of current item**: $[Y]
**Net cost of upgrade**: $[X-Y]

**Actual new capabilities** (features I'll use that current item can't do): [Count]
**Cost per new capability**: $[Net cost] / [Capabilities] = $[Z]

**Expected uses per month**: [Realistic number]
**Cost per use over expected lifetime**: $[Calculate]

## The Downgrade Test
**If I lost my current item, would I**:
- [ ] Buy the exact same one again
- [ ] Buy the cheaper version
- [ ] Buy this "upgrade"
- [ ] Not replace it at all

**Why this matters**: If you wouldn't buy the upgrade from zero, you don't really want it.

## The Satisfaction Paradox
**Current satisfaction**: [Score/10]
**Expected satisfaction after upgrade**: [Score/10]
**Expected satisfaction gain**: [Difference]

**Reality check from past upgrades**:
- Last upgrade: [Item] → [Item]
- Expected satisfaction gain: [Score]
- Actual satisfaction gain after 6 months: [Score]
- Pattern: [What this tells you]

**Research finding**: Most upgrades provide < 1 month of satisfaction boost before returning to baseline.

## Opportunity Cost
**$[Net cost] could alternatively buy**:
1. [Alternative use of money]
2. [Alternative use of money]
3. [Alternative use of money]

**Which would provide more lasting value?**: [Honest assessment]

## Decision
**I will**: [BUY/NOT BUY]
**Because**: [Reason based on actual value, not aspirational use]

**If buying**: 
- Max price: $[Amount]
- Features that matter: [Specific list]
- Success metric: [How I'll measure if this was worth it]

## 6-Month Review
[Set reminder for DATE]
- Actual satisfaction vs expected: [Compare]
- Features used vs purchased for: [Compare]
- Was this worth it?: [YES/NO + Why]
- What did I learn about my upgrade patterns?
```

## 6. Pre-Commitment Post-Mortem

**When to use**: Before saying yes to ongoing commitments (projects, relationships, obligations)

**Template**:

```markdown
# Pre-Commitment Post-Mortem: [Commitment Name]

## The Commitment
**What**: [Specific description]
**Time required**: [Hours/week or total hours]
**Duration**: [Start date] to [End date or ongoing]
**Why I'm considering**: [Honest reason]

## The Future Scenario
**It's now 6 months from today**. I've stopped doing this commitment.

**Why did I quit?** (Be brutally honest about likely reasons)
1. [Reason based on past patterns]
2. [Reason based on past patterns]
3. [Reason based on past patterns]

## Pattern Recognition
**Similar past commitments**:

| Commitment | Duration Planned | Duration Actual | Why I Stopped |
|------------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------|
| [Example 1] | [Time] | [Time] | [Reason] |
| [Example 2] | [Time] | [Time] | [Reason] |
| [Example 3] | [Time] | [Time] | [Reason] |

**Pattern identified**: [What reliably makes me quit?]

## The Failure Pre-Planning
**Given my pattern, this commitment will likely fail because**:
- [Specific vulnerability based on pattern]
- [Specific vulnerability based on pattern]

**How I'll prevent each failure mode**:
- [Failure mode 1] → [Specific prevention strategy]
- [Failure mode 2] → [Specific prevention strategy]

**If I can't articulate concrete prevention strategies, I should not commit.**

## The Obligation Audit
**What I'm obligating myself to**:
- [Specific deliverable or behavior]
- [Specific deliverable or behavior]

**What happens if I quit halfway**:
- Who gets hurt: [Specific people]
- What gets damaged: [Specific consequences]
- How hard to extract myself: [Easy/Medium/Hard]

**Exit strategy**: [Specific plan for graceful exit if needed]

## The Enthusiasm Decay Curve
**Current enthusiasm** (1-10): [Score]
**Expected enthusiasm in 1 month**: [Score]
**Expected enthusiasm in 3 months**: [Score]
**Expected enthusiasm in 6 months**: [Score]

**At what enthusiasm level will I quit?**: [Threshold]
**When will I likely hit that threshold?**: [Timeframe]

**Reality check**: Am I willing to continue this commitment even when enthusiasm hits [Threshold]?

## The Replacement Test
**What I'll stop doing to make room for this**:
- [Activity]: From [X hours] to [Y hours]

**Can I live with that trade for 6 months?**: [YES/NO]
**What will I miss most about the thing I'm reducing?**: [Specific answer]

## Decision
**I will commit**: [YES/NO/DEFER]

**If YES**:
- [ ] I've identified my likely failure modes
- [ ] I have concrete prevention strategies
- [ ] I have a clear exit strategy
- [ ] I've made realistic time trades
- [ ] I'm willing to continue even when enthusiasm drops

**If any box unchecked**: Revise or decline.

## Check-in Schedule
- **Week 2**: [Reminder] - Is enthusiasm still high? Any early warning signs?
- **Month 1**: [Reminder] - Check failure modes. Are preventions working?
- **Month 3**: [Reminder] - Enthusiasm check. Still worth it?
- **Month 6**: [Reminder] - Full post-mortem. What actually happened vs prediction?
```
