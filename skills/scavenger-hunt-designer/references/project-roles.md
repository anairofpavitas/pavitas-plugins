# Project roles: what to find

Every clue's find fills a role in the player's project. The role connects back to one or more creative seeds where it can. Use this catalog as a starting palette. Invent new roles when the project is unusual.

## Decorating a space (party, ofrenda, dinner, room)

- **Centerpiece.** The visual anchor of the space.
- **Overhead.** Something to hang — garland, banner, mobile, string light.
- **Vessels.** Vases, bowls, jars, anything that holds something else.
- **Scent.** Candle, incense, herb bundle, flowers.
- **Lighting.** Beyond the overhead — table candles, lanterns, fairy lights.
- **Scatter.** Small repeated objects — confetti, petals, pebbles, charms.
- **The weird object.** One unexpected piece that breaks the theme on purpose.
- **Foraged greenery.** Branches, leaves, herbs, weeds — found, not bought.
- **Signage.** A label, a menu, a sign, a name card.
- **Runner.** Fabric, paper, ribbon — a line through the space.

## Gathering supplies for a craft

- **Substrate.** What you build on or attach to.
- **Binder.** Glue, thread, wire, tape — whatever holds it together.
- **Tool.** Something you don't have at home that the craft needs.
- **Treasure.** The hero element. The thing the craft is built around.
- **Contrast material.** Something opposite to the dominant texture or color.
- **Finish.** Varnish, paint, sealant, fabric stiffener, wax.
- **Salvage piece.** Something with prior life — vintage, broken, secondhand.

## Assembling a gift bag for a person

- **Something edible.** Treats, snacks, a single fancy thing.
- **Something useful.** Anchored in what the recipient actually does.
- **Something purely beautiful.** No function. Just lovely.
- **The card.** Handmade or store-bought, but specific to them.
- **The note.** Written that day, by hand, in the bag.
- **The wrap.** Bag, tissue, ribbon, the whole presentation.
- **An inside joke.** Only the giver and recipient get it.
- **Something nostalgic.** Tied to a specific memory you share.

## Putting together an outfit

- **The anchor.** The defining piece — jacket, dress, shoes, hat.
- **Head.** Hat, headband, scarf, sunglasses, earrings.
- **Feet.** Shoes, socks, anklets.
- **Layer.** Cardigan, vest, scarf, jacket.
- **Finisher.** The last small thing — pin, ring, pocket square.
- **Enhancement.** Modify something a player is already wearing — a tie, a fold, a pin added to the lapel.

## Food / meal projects

The project might be one shared meal, a charcuterie or grazing board, a picnic spread, multiple individual dishes under a theme, or a built kit (ingredients for someone to cook later).

- **Hero ingredient.** The dish or board is built around it.
- **Aromatic.** Herb, allium, citrus zest — the smell layer.
- **Acid.** Vinegar, citrus, pickled thing, sour element.
- **Crunch.** A texture contrast — crackers, nuts, a fried something.
- **Fat.** Cheese, oil, butter, cured meat, avocado.
- **Sweet element.** Fruit, jam, honey, candied something.
- **Starch / bread.** The carrier or the base.
- **Drink.** Bottle, can, jug — pairs with the meal.
- **Garnish.** The final visual flourish.
- **Serving vessel.** Platter, board, basket, plates, napkins.

Project × mode notes:
- One shared meal or board: cooperative.
- Multiple individual dishes under a theme (everyone makes their version): competitive viable. Iron Chef vibe.
- A picnic spread eaten together: cooperative.
- A kit for someone else to cook later: cooperative.

Allergy or dietary restrictions get logged at intake under "anything else worth knowing" and baked into the clue text. Don't send a vegetarian to find cured meat.

## Memory / sentimental projects

The project might be an ofrenda, an anniversary scrapbook, a memorial bouquet, a tribute box, an annual remembrance kit, materials for a ceremony of any kind.

**The design philosophy shifts here.** Still walking, still collecting, but:

- Fewer races. The pace should feel like a deliberate walk, not a chase.
- Fewer stranger interactions. Some people don't want to perform grief for an audience.
- Photo prompts read warmer, not jokier. "A candid of the moment everyone agrees on" — not "the silliest pose with the candle."
- Pit Stops matter more — give the group time to talk, not just hydrate.
- The finale is the assembled object, staged with care. The keeper shot is reverent.
- Optional challenges should match the tone. No "build a five-dollar outfit" sidequests.
- Tone in the clue text: warm, direct, no jokes that don't land in the room.

Possible roles:

- **An object that represents them.** The hero of the project.
- **Something in their color.** A shade tied to them.
- **Something that smells like them.** A cologne, a soap, a kitchen herb, a flower they grew.
- **A flower or plant they loved.** Or a found stem from where you walk.
- **A scrap from this place.** A pebble, a ticket, a pressed leaf — proof you walked here for them.
- **A favorite of theirs.** A food, a drink, a small thing they always had on them.
- **An offering.** Something to leave at the assembled spot — coin, candle, written note, photograph.

Project × mode notes:
- Building one memorial or shrine together: cooperative.
- Multiple individual tributes under a shared theme: parallel-solo, but stay close and reconvene at the finale.
- Never make this competitive. The mode/project mismatch is too sharp.

If the user names this kind of project at intake, gently confirm the tone they want — some people want reverent, some want celebratory, some want a mix. Honor what they ask for.

## Anything else

Invent the roles. A care package needs different roles than a sourdough starter kit needs different roles than a busking outfit. Sketch them by asking:

- What does this project need to be complete?
- What's the centerpiece — the one thing the whole project is built around?
- What's the supporting cast — the things that frame the centerpiece?
- What's the unexpected element — the thing that makes it specific to this person, this day, this group?

Each role should connect to a creative seed where it can — and stand on its own when it can't.
