# Boundary tiers

Every activity in a pleasure hunt sits in one of four tiers, by barrier of entry. The user names which tiers are on the table at intake. A hunt escalates through the tiers in play — opening low, climbing toward the ceiling.

These are the user's own taxonomy. Use them as given. If the user reclassifies an activity, follow the new placement.

---

## Low — low barrier of entry

## How a hunt climbs tiers

The ceiling the user names is a **cap, not a target**. A hunt with "high" on the table can peak at medium if that's where the energy lands.

- **Open** one or two tiers below the ceiling. A high-tier hunt opens low or medium.
- **Build** by moving up roughly one tier across the middle clues. Don't jump low straight to extreme.
- **Peak** at the ceiling — or below it. The top clue is permission, not obligation.

If only one tier is on the table, escalation happens *within* it. Low can climb from a clothed touch to a hand down the pants without ever leaving the tier. Medium can climb from a 20-second taste to unhurried mutual oral.

A generated hunt never escalates past the named ceiling. If the moment wants more, that's the user's call to make live — see principle 1 in SKILL.md.

## Tier and mode

Not every tier fits every mode cleanly:

- **Solo** reaches every tier, including the whole extreme tier.
- **Partner** reaches every tier with a trusted partner.
- **Group** lives mostly low through high; extreme is rare and only with explicit setup.
- **Cruising** lives low through high; the extreme tier almost never fits a stranger encounter. Public/semi-public clues stay low to medium.
