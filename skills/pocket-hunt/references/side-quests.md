# Side quests

Optional beats that can be offered between clue stretches. Always skippable. Never more than one per hunt unless the user requests another.

## When to offer a side quest

- User has been hunting for >30 minutes
- No side quest in this hunt yet
- Context fits the specific side quest's conditions
- The hunt has at least 20 more minutes of runway (don't side-quest in the closing stretch)

If conditions don't fit any side quest, generate the next regular clue. Don't force.

## How to offer

One line, between clue stretches:

> *"Optional side quest: [name]. Want in, or keep going as planned?"*

Brief. Skippable. No pressure. If declined, generate next regular clue. If accepted, the side quest replaces or augments the next 1–3 clues, then the regular hunt resumes.

## The catalog

### Person you know in the area

**Conditions:** mid-to-late hunt, commercial activity nearby, hasn't been deployed yet.

**Pitch:** *"Optional side quest. Do you know anyone who lives near where you are right now?"*

**If yes:** suggest building a mini gift bag for them based on what's findable in the current environment. 3–5 small items. Then deliver — knock unannounced or text first, user's call. If recipient isn't home, save the bag for next meeting.

**If no:** skip, generate next regular clue.

This side quest can also become the main shape mid-hunt if the user wants — see `hunt-shapes.md` for the destination-with-delivery transition.

### Snack / drink / coffee / candy break

**Conditions:** any environment with food access nearby, no recent pit stop, user has been moving for 30+ min.

**Variations to rotate:**

- **Coffee break.** *"Find a coffee shop you haven't been to before — or your favorite. Order something with a flavor you don't usually pick. Sit for 10 minutes."*
- **Snack break.** *"Get a snack. Something specific: a single piece of fruit, one pastry, a small bag of chips. Eat it on a bench you find on the way."*
- **Candy.** *"Find a corner store, deli, or specialty candy shop. Get one piece of candy you remember from childhood — or one you've never tried before."*
- **Bar pause.** *"Find a bar with a sit-down spot in the window or patio. One drink (alcoholic or not, your call). 15 minutes."*
- **Farmers market detour.** *"Is there a farmers market within a reasonable walk? Stop in. Buy one small thing — a piece of fruit, a single flower, a small jar of something. **Bonus +5 points if you actually buy fruit** — count it as a built-in seed for the rest of the hunt."*

Pick based on time of day, weather, what the user mentioned wanting, or randomize from the appropriate set.

### Found-object micro-mission

**Conditions:** environment has natural elements (parks, residential streets with gardens, trails), rung 2–4.

**Pitch:** *"Optional side quest: micro-foraging. Next three blocks (or 10 minutes), find one stone, one leaf, one piece of bark. Keep them for later — they might land in the closing arrangement."*

Variation themes:
- One of each: stone, leaf, bark
- Three of the same kind in different sizes (three leaves, three pebbles, three sticks)
- One specimen for each seed color
- A "color collection" — five items, each a different color, all small enough to fit in a pocket

### Conversation pivot

**Conditions:** human companion engaged, hasn't been used yet this hunt.

**Pitch:** *"Optional side quest: pause the hunt for a real conversation. Ask [companion] something you haven't asked recently. Sit with it for 5–10 minutes before the next clue."*

Conversation starters to rotate:
- *"What's been on your mind that you haven't said out loud yet?"*
- *"What's a small thing you're looking forward to this week?"*
- *"What's something you noticed earlier that you didn't mention?"*
- *"What's an old memory you've been thinking about?"*
- *"What's something you'd like more of in your life right now?"*

If user prefers a lighter touch: *"Just talk for 10 minutes. Anything. Skip the next clue prompt."*

### Pit stop interrupt

**Conditions:** user has been moving for 40+ min without a real sit, or weather/energy suggests rest needed.

**Pitch:** *"You've been moving for a while. Optional side quest: pit stop. Find a bench, cafe, or quiet spot. Sit for 10 minutes before the next clue."*

Simple, unscripted. Sometimes the side quest is *stop hunting briefly*.

### Photo theme detour

**Conditions:** rung 2+, photos already in play.

**Pitch:** *"Optional side quest: the next three things you photograph all have to share a single specific quality. Pick one: same color, same texture, same shape, same size, same emotion. Then resume the regular hunt."*

Adds a constraint, makes the looking sharper. Particularly nice in residential environments.

### Reverse hunt

**Conditions:** mid-to-late hunt, user is engaged, wants more control.

**Pitch:** *"Optional side quest: you write the next clue. Tell me where to send you and what to do there. I'll trust you."*

User pitches the next clue. Skill executes it. Returns to regular generation after.

### Architecture deep-dive

**Conditions:** residential or commercial-historic environment.

**Pitch:** *"Optional side quest: pick one building within sight. Spend 5 minutes looking at it — really looking. Find three things about it you wouldn't have noticed at a glance. Photograph one of them."*

Particularly nice in older Chicago neighborhoods. Architecture-rich, walking-pleasant.

### Stranger watching (no interaction)

**Conditions:** moderate foot traffic, user is in a comfortable spot.

**Pitch:** *"Optional side quest: sit on a bench or in a window seat for 10 minutes. Don't approach anyone, don't interact. Just watch. Notice three things about three different people that no one else would pick up on."*

Pure noticing, no social pressure. Works as a presence sharpener.

### Dog choice

**Conditions:** dog companions present.

**Pitch:** *"Optional side quest: the next 15 minutes, the dogs lead. Wherever they want to sniff, you sniff (figuratively). Wherever they pick to stop, you stop. Photograph what they investigate."*

Cedes control to the dogs. Often produces unexpected directions. Pavi's dogs (Jimothy, Linen) will probably love it.

## Adding new side quests

This file is designed to grow. As Pavi runs hunts and notices what works, new side quests can be added here without touching SKILL.md.

When proposing a new side quest, include:

1. **Name** — short, memorable
2. **Conditions** — when to offer it
3. **Pitch** — the one-line prompt
4. **Variations** — if applicable
5. **Notes** — what makes it work, what to watch for
