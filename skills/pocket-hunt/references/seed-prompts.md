# Seed prompts

Seeds in pocket-hunt are discovered through **prompted attention exercises**, not user-named themes. The prompt directs the user's attention somewhere specific, asks them to do small noticing work, and then to name what they found. The seed is the output of the noticing.

The user can always override and name their own seed directly. Prompts are the default; override is the escape hatch.

Generate 3 seed prompts per hunt, varied patterns, adapted to environment, rung, and companion state.

---

## Solo patterns

For solo hunts and for companion hunts where the companion is "along for the walk but not playing."

### Echo prompt
Find something in the environment that matches something on or with you. Color, texture, shape, material.

- *"Look at what you're wearing right now. Find an object in your sight line that shares its dominant color. Notice the object's shape. That's your seed."*
- *"You're carrying something — bag, wallet, phone case, scarf. Find a building, sign, or surface within a block that echoes its texture. That's your seed."*

### Synesthetic pairing
Two sensory channels collide. Sound + object. Smell + color. Texture + sound.

- *"Stop for 10 seconds. What's the most distinct sound you can hear? Now find an object nearby that looks like it could make that sound — even if it can't. Pair the sound with the misattributed object. That's your seed."*
- *"What does this block smell like? Find something within sight that should have that smell but doesn't, or doesn't have it but should. That's your seed."*

### Time-shift
The same place across time. The oldest and the newest. The has-been and the will-be.

- *"Find the oldest visible thing in your sight line. Now find the newest. Both at once are your seed."*
- *"Look at this place. What did it look like 50 years ago? Find one thing that's been here that long, and one thing that hasn't. Those are your seeds."*

### Misattribution
A sign or label, pretended to mean something else.

- *"Find a sign within a block. Pretend the words mean something completely different than what they actually mean. Your seed is that wrong meaning."*
- *"Pick the name of a business near you. What if it was actually a different kind of business? Your seed is the imagined version."*

### The thing nobody else looked at
A specific instinct: find something that has been *unwitnessed*.

- *"Pick one thing on this block — a detail, a small object, a piece of a building — that you're 90% sure no one else has looked at today. That's your seed."*
- *"What's directly above your head right now? Look up. Whatever's there, that's your seed."*

### Negative space
What should be there but isn't.

- *"Find the most empty spot in your sight line. Your seed is what should be there but isn't — what you'd put there if you could."*
- *"Look for an absence. A missing tile, a faded paint patch, a closed-up doorway. Your seed is the gap."*

### Mood translation
A feeling translated into a physical thing.

- *"How are you feeling right now? Find an object within a block that looks like that feeling. That's your seed."*
- *"What kind of light is it right now — what's the quality of it? Find an object that holds that light differently than the surroundings. That's your seed."*

### One word
A single word picked from environment, used as a generative anchor.

- *"Find one word in your environment — sign, label, t-shirt, graffiti. The first one that catches your eye. That word is your seed."*
- *"Listen for a snatch of conversation as you walk. The first noun you hear someone say. That noun is your seed."*

## Companion conversation prompts

For hunts where the human companion is engaged at rung 2+. The format shifts: the prompt is read silently by the user, who then brings what they noticed into natural conversation. The conversation produces the seed, not the prompt directly.

These prompts should never be read aloud at the companion. They're internal direction; the output is what surfaces in talk.

### Memory anchor
Find something that triggers a memory tied to the companion. Bring it into conversation.

- *"Find something in your sight line that reminds you of an early memory with [companion]. Bring it into conversation — 'this reminds me of...' — and let the talk unfold. The seed is whatever the conversation surfaces."*
- *"Look around. What's the first object you see that connects to a specific date or trip you've taken together? Mention it. See where they take it."*

### Hypothetical projection
If the companion could take one thing from here home, what would it be? Find it for them in spirit.

- *"If [companion] could take one thing from this block home with them, what would it be? Find it. Tell them you found their thing. See what they say."*
- *"What's the one shop or place on this block [companion] would most want to spend an hour in? Find out by asking what they'd do here for an hour."*

### Past-place anchor
This place vs. another place from your shared past.

- *"What does this block remind you of from somewhere you've both been? Tell them. Ask what it reminds them of."*
- *"What kind of place is this? Is it more like [a place you've been together] or [a different place you've been together]? Ask them."*

### Sensory observation about them
What's something here that *they'd* notice but you might not?

- *"What's something in this environment [companion] would notice that you'd miss? Find it. Point it out — 'I knew you'd see this.'"*
- *"What kind of smell, sound, or detail is uniquely 'their' kind of thing on this block? Mention it without naming the prompt."*

### Mild reveal
Find something that represents something you've been thinking about but haven't said.

- *"Find something here that represents something you've been thinking about lately but haven't told [companion]. You don't have to tell them what — just notice it, and let the talk go where it goes."*

### Future-pace
Find something you'd want to come back to with them in five years.

- *"Find something here you'd want to come back to with [companion] in five years. Tell them. Make a small future-plan around it, even if it's silly."*

### Feeling-of-the-place
Rhetorical anchor.

- *"If this whole block was a feeling, what would it be? Ask [companion] what they'd call it. The conversation is the seed."*
- *"What kind of day does this place seem like it's having? Ask them."*

## Companion + dog prompts

When dogs are present, seed prompts adapt:

- The walk pace is dictated by the dogs' sniffing and stopping
- Photo prompts skew toward the dogs' experience (the dog watching, the dog choosing, the dog napping)
- Training-context prompts are welcome — *"find a bench with moderate foot traffic and do a 2-minute sit/watch session. Photograph the dog's best calm moment."*
- Found-object prompts pair well with sniff-walking — *"on the next three blocks, the dog will pick a stopping point at each. At each, find one small thing to bring home."*

The dog *is* a companion. The seed prompts can address the dog's role explicitly — *"what's something on this block that's only at dog-eye-level? Crouch and look."*

## Generating a seed set

For a 3-seed set, vary the patterns. Don't pick three of the same kind. A good mix:

- One environment-anchored (echo, time-shift, misattribution)
- One mood/sense-anchored (synesthetic, mood translation, one word)
- One people-anchored (negative space, nobody-else-looked, or — if companion — a conversation prompt)

If the rung is 3 or 4 (acquisition), bias toward seed prompts that produce concrete, transferable themes — colors, textures, materials, silhouettes. Acquisition needs anchors the user can match against shop inventory.

If the rung is 1 (notice only), bias toward seed prompts that produce abstract or unfixed themes — moods, sounds, absences. Noticing thrives on ambiguity.

---

## Override

Always available. The user can name their own seeds at any point. Example: *"Skip the prompts — my seeds are: rust-orange, ceramic texture, the word 'somewhere.'"* Accept and proceed. The override is the user's authority, full stop.

If the user overrides partially — names one seed, asks for prompts for the other two — generate the remaining prompts and combine.
